üîç Six Types of Analysis Performed

1. Change Over Time

Tracked sales trends across months and years to identify seasonal patterns and growth trajectories. Analyzed total sales, customer counts, and quantity sold over time periods.

2. Cumulative Analysis

Calculated running totals and moving averages using window functions to understand cumulative performance and momentum in sales growth.

3. Performance Analysis

Evaluated year-over-year product performance by comparing current sales to historical averages and previous year figures to identify winners and losers.

4. Part-to-Whole Proportional

Determined which product categories contribute most to overall revenue through percentage calculations, enabling focused inventory and marketing strategies.

5. Data Segmentation

Segmented customers (VIP/Regular/New) and products (High/Mid/Low performers) based on behavioral metrics and revenue thresholds for targeted actions.

6. Comprehensive Reporting

Created reusable SQL views that consolidate all metrics into dashboard-ready reports for customer and product analytics.

üíª Technical Implementation

Data Architecture

The project follows a star schema data warehouse design:

  • Fact Table: fact_sales (transaction-level sales data)
  • Dimension Tables: dim_customers, dim_products
  • Reporting Layer: gold_report_customers, gold_report_products (aggregated views)

Sample Query: Customer Segmentation

-- Segment customers into VIP, Regular, and New WITH customer_spending AS ( SELECT f.customer_key, SUM(f.sales_amount) AS total_spending, TIMESTAMPDIFF(MONTH, MIN(f.order_date), MAX(f.order_date)) AS lifespan FROM fact_sales f WHERE f.order_date IS NOT NULL GROUP BY f.customer_key ) SELECT CASE WHEN lifespan >= 12 AND total_spending > 5000 THEN 'VIP' WHEN lifespan >= 12 AND total_spending <= 5000 THEN 'Regular' ELSE 'New' END AS customer_segment, COUNT(customer_key) AS total_customers FROM customer_spending GROUP BY customer_segment;
